Machine Guns

Heavy automatic firearms used primarily by military forces for suppression and area denial.

M249 SAW
USA LMG 5.56mm

A lightweight belt-fed machine gun providing squad-level suppressive fire.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 800m (Point)
  • Weight: 7.5 kg (Empty)
  • Length: 1035mm
  • Feed: Belt / STANAG
HISTORY & SERVICE

Adopted by the US Military in 1984, the M249 Squad Automatic Weapon (SAW) filled the gap between the M16 rifle and the M60 machine gun. It allows a single infantryman to provide the volume of fire of a heavy machine gun with the portability of a rifle.

PKM
RUSSIA GPMG 7.62x54R

A legendary general-purpose machine gun known for extreme durability.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 1000m
  • Weight: 7.5 kg
  • Length: 1160mm
  • Action: Gas-operated
HISTORY & SERVICE

Designed by Mikhail Kalashnikov, the PKM combines the bolt mechanism of the AK-47 with the belt-feed of the RPD. It is considered one of the best machine guns ever made due to its incredibly low weight for a full-caliber weapon.

FN MAG
BELGIUM GPMG 7.62 NATO

The gold standard for NATO machine guns, used by over 80 countries.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 1200m
  • Weight: 11.8 kg
  • Length: 1260mm
  • Feed: M13 Link Belt
HISTORY & SERVICE

The FN MAG 58 operates using a long-stroke gas piston similar to the BAR. Its robust riveted steel receiver makes it heavy but virtually indestructible, serving as the primary GPMG for the US (M240), UK (L7), and many others.

MG3
GERMANY GPMG 7.62 NATO

A modern evolution of the WWII MG42 "Buzzsaw".

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 1200m
  • Weight: 11.5 kg
  • Length: 1225mm
  • Op: Roller-delayed
HISTORY & SERVICE

Derived directly from the MG42, the MG3 retains the terrifying 1,200 rounds-per-minute fire rate. It is primarily used as a vehicle-mounted weapon or for static defense, as the recoil and ammo consumption are difficult for foot soldiers.

RPK
RUSSIA LMG 7.62x39mm

Essentially a heavy-barreled AK-47 designed for sustained fire.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 800m
  • Weight: 4.8 kg
  • Length: 1040mm
  • Feed: Mag / Drum
HISTORY & SERVICE

The RPK was designed to standardize the infantry squad on the AK platform. It features a longer, heavier barrel, a bipod, and a reinforced receiver. It accepts standard AK magazines, making logistics incredibly simple.

Negev NG7
ISRAEL LMG 7.62 NATO

The only light machine gun capable of semi-automatic fire.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 1000m
  • Weight: 7.6 kg
  • Length: 1000mm
  • Mode: Semi/Auto
HISTORY & SERVICE

Developed by IWI, the Negev is unique for its selective fire capability. This allows soldiers to use it as a DMR (Designated Marksman Rifle) for single shots or a suppression weapon in full auto, ideal for urban combat.

M60
USA GPMG 7.62 NATO

"The Pig" - An iconic heavy hitter from the Vietnam era.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 1100m
  • Weight: 10.5 kg
  • Length: 1105mm
  • Feed: Disintegrating Link
HISTORY & SERVICE

Known affectionately (and sometimes derisively) as "The Pig" due to its bulk and hunger for ammo, the M60 defined US firepower in Vietnam. Its slow rate of fire makes it extremely controllable, allowing for walking fire.

Kord
RUSSIA HMG 12.7x108mm

A massive 12.7mm beast designed to destroy light vehicles.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 2000m
  • Weight: 25.5 kg
  • Length: 1980mm
  • Role: Anti-Material
HISTORY & SERVICE

The Kord replaced the older NSV machine gun. Its most impressive feature is its advanced recoil system, which allows this heavy 12.7mm weapon to be fired accurately from a bipod—something most heavy machine guns cannot do.

Type 88
CHINA HMG 12.7x108mm

A versatile Chinese HMG often deployed with a tripod.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 1500m
  • Weight: 11.8 kg
  • Length: 1500mm
  • Mount: Tripod/Vehicle
HISTORY & SERVICE

The QJZ-89 (Type 88) was designed to be the lightest 12.7mm heavy machine gun in the world. It can be carried and deployed by a three-man infantry crew, giving small units massive firepower usually reserved for vehicles.

L7A2
UK GPMG 7.62 NATO

The British Army's reliable workhorse for decades.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 800m
  • Weight: 11 kg
  • Length: 1230mm
  • Role: Section Support
HISTORY & SERVICE

A licensed variant of the FN MAG, the "Gimpy" (General Purpose Machine Gun) is beloved by British troops for its reliability. It has served in every major British conflict from the Falklands to Afghanistan.

M2 Browning
USA HMG .50 BMG

"Ma Deuce" - Serving the US Military for nearly 100 years.

PERFORMANCE STATS
TECHNICAL SPECS
  • Range: 1800m+
  • Weight: 38 kg (Gun)
  • Length: 1654mm
  • Feed: M2/M9 Link
HISTORY & SERVICE

Designed by John Moses Browning in 1918, the M2HB is the longest-serving weapon in US history. Its .50 BMG round can tear through engine blocks, brick walls, and light armor, making it the ultimate battlefield problem solver.
